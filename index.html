Services

What we do

We make Home Medicines Reviews simple for patients, efficient for GPs and professionally rewarding for accredited pharmacists.

For patients & carers

Home Medicines Review at home

A credentialed pharmacist visits you at home to review all your medicines – prescriptions, over‑the‑counter products and supplements – and helps you understand how they work together in your day‑to‑day life.

For GPs

HMR / DMMR support (item 900)

We streamline referrals, coordinate the pharmacist visit and provide concise, actionable reports to support your medication management plans and quality use of medicines, in line with national program requirements.

For pharmacists

Consultant pharmacist partnerships

We match accredited pharmacists with suitable referrals, support efficient workflows and encourage strong clinical communication with referring GPs so that reviews stay clinically meaningful.

How it works

From referral to safer medicines

A clear, GP‑led process that keeps the patient and their usual healthcare team at the centre.

1
GP identifies the need

The GP or practice nurse identifies a patient at risk of medication‑related problems and discusses a Home Medicines Review with them, gaining informed consent.

2
Referral to Medication Review Australia

The GP sends a referral via their preferred secure channel. We confirm the details and allocate a credentialed pharmacist in the patient’s local area.

3
Pharmacist home visit

The pharmacist visits the patient at home, reviews all medicines in the context of their real routine and answers questions about what each medication is really for and how to take it safely.

4
Report & GP follow‑up

A written report goes back to the GP, who updates the patient’s medication management plan in a follow‑up appointment and, where appropriate, claims MBS item 900 for the completed DMMR.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions from patients, carers, GPs and pharmacists about Home Medicines Reviews.

What is a Home Medicines Review?
A Home Medicines Review (HMR), also called a Domiciliary Medication Management Review (DMMR), is a structured review of a patient’s medicines. The GP refers the patient to an accredited pharmacist, who visits the patient at home, reviews all medicines and sends a report back to the GP.
Does it cost me anything as a patient?
For most eligible patients with a Medicare or DVA card, the pharmacist’s review is funded under national medication management programs and the GP may claim MBS item 900 once the process is complete. Your GP’s usual billing policies for consultations may still apply.
How do I know if I’m eligible?
Your GP will decide. You’re more likely to be eligible if you live in the community and have a chronic condition, take multiple regular medicines, have recently left hospital or have had major changes to your medicines.
Do you replace my GP or regular pharmacy?
No. Your GP remains responsible for your medical care and your usual pharmacy continues to supply your medicines. Medication Review Australia coordinates the extra step of having a credentialed pharmacist review your medicines and report back to your GP.
