About Us

Who we are

Medication Review is a dedicated home medicines review service helping Australians use their medicines safely and confidently at home.

We coordinate GP-led Home Medicines Reviews (HMR / DMMR), connecting general practices with accredited pharmacists who review real-life medication use in the patient’s home. Our goal is simple: safer medicines, fewer problems, and more patients who understand exactly what they’re taking and why.

Our philosophy

Because each pill should have a purpose.

We believe every tablet, capsule, inhaler and drop should earn its place in a person’s day. That means:

  • Patients know what each medicine is really for
  • GPs have clear, practical information to guide decisions
  • Pharmacists are recognised as key medicines experts in the care team

How it works

From referral to review

A clear, GP-led process that keeps the patient and their usual healthcare team at the centre.

1
GP identifies the need

The GP or practice nurse identifies a patient at risk of medication-related problems and discusses a Home Medicines Review with them, gaining consent.

2
Referral to Medication Review

The GP sends a referral through their preferred channel. We confirm the details and allocate a credentialed pharmacist in the patient’s area.

3
Pharmacist home visit

The pharmacist visits the patient at home, reviews all medicines in the context of their real routine and answers questions about what each medication is really for.

4
Report & follow-up

A written report goes back to the GP, who updates the patient’s medication plan in a follow-up consult and, where appropriate, claims MBS item 900 for the completed DMMR.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to some of the questions we hear most from patients, carers, GPs and pharmacists.

What is a Home Medicines Review?
A Home Medicines Review (HMR), also called a Domiciliary Medication Management Review (DMMR), is a structured review of your medicines. Your GP refers you to an accredited pharmacist, who visits you at home, reviews all your medicines and sends a report back to your GP.
Does it cost me anything as a patient?
For most eligible patients with a Medicare or DVA card, the pharmacist’s review is funded under national medication management programs and your GP may claim MBS item 900 once the process is complete. Your GP’s usual billing policies for consultations may still apply.
How do I know if I’m eligible?
Your GP will decide. You’re more likely to be eligible if you live in the community and have a chronic condition, take multiple regular medicines, have recently left hospital or have had major changes to your medicines.
Do you replace my GP or regular pharmacy?
No. Your GP remains responsible for your medical care and your usual pharmacy continues to supply your medicines. Medication Review simply coordinates the extra step of having a credentialed pharmacist review your medicines and report back to your GP.
