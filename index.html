What is a Home Medicines Review?

A full picture of every medicine, at home

A Home Medicines Review (HMR) is a structured consultation where an accredited pharmacist visits the patient at home to review all of their medicines.

The pharmacist looks at prescriptions, over-the-counter products and supplements, checks how they are being taken, and discusses any side-effects, confusion or practical difficulties. A written report then goes back to the referring GP, who updates the patient’s medication management plan.

The goal is simple: reduce medication-related harm, improve treatment effectiveness, and give patients confidence in the medicines they use every day.

How the referral process works

From identification to Medicare claim

A familiar, GP-led process that keeps the usual care team at the centre.

1
GP identifies the need

The GP or practice nurse recognises a patient at risk of medication-related problems and discusses a Home Medicines Review with them, gaining informed consent.

2
Referral to Medication Review Australia

The GP sends a referral via HealthLink ID medrview, fax 03 9022 7830 or email hmr@mymedicationreview.com.au. We confirm details and allocate a credentialed pharmacist in the patient’s area.

3
Pharmacist home visit

An accredited pharmacist visits the patient at home, reviews all medicines in the context of their real routine and answers questions about how to take them safely.

4
Report & GP follow-up

A written report goes back to the GP, who updates the patient’s medication management plan in a follow-up appointment and discusses any changes.

5
MBS Item 900

Once the review and follow-up are complete, the GP may claim the Domiciliary Medication Management Review under MBS Item 900, in line with current program rules.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Common questions about Home Medicines Reviews from patients, carers, GPs and pharmacists.

Is there a cost to the patient?
For most eligible patients with a Medicare or DVA card, the pharmacist’s review is funded under national medication management programs and the GP may claim MBS Item 900 once the process is complete. Your GP’s usual billing policies for consultations may still apply.
Do you replace my GP or regular pharmacy?
No. Your GP remains responsible for your medical care and your usual pharmacy continues to supply your medicines. Medication Review Australia coordinates the additional step of having a credentialed pharmacist review your medicines and report back to your GP.
How long does an HMR appointment take?
The home visit usually takes 45–90 minutes, depending on the number of medicines and the complexity of your health conditions. After the visit, the pharmacist prepares a report for your GP.
How do I request an HMR for myself or a family member?
Start by speaking with your GP, who will determine whether an HMR is appropriate and organise a referral. You can also email us at hmr@mymedicationreview.com.au if you have questions about eligibility or the process.
